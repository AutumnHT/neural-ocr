Neural Network OCR

neural-ocr is a feed-forward neural network with back-propagation learning that is used for optical character recognition (OCR). It was trained and tested using normalized images derived from the MNIST database of handwritten digits.

Tuning the various parameters (number of layers, height of layers, activation threshold, bias weight, etc.) allows us to achieve a maximum correct recognition rate of approximately 35%. This is low because of the small amount of training data - the MNIST dataset offers only 1000 images of each digit. In a practical application, a training dataset of millions of images would result in much higher recognition rates. Regardless, the recognition rate for a purely random decision machine would be only 10%, so consistently achieving rates of higher than 30% is statistically significant.

These results, as well as the detailed design and implementation of the neural network, are described in depth in the final paper.
